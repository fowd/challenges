F1.1 - Content Development

Description & Requirements

It's now time to start developing your final project! The first step will be to gather and develop the content for the project—A portfolio website. If you're not an artist, you can develop a site promoting some other hobby, family, or business.

Your final site should have the following pages/sections:

  • Home page (index.html): This page may have a brief (artist/mission) statement, artwork/work, or other introductory material
  • Contact page (contact.php): You will have a contact form you will develop in the next module, allowing visitors to the site to send you an email ... you will also have a thank you page, after the form is submitted
  • A page (or pages) with your main material ... this could be a portfolio gallery, your ministry, or product; each page should be named accordingly (i.e. gallery.html, ministry.html, widgets.html)
  • A page that talks more about you (about.html) ... If you don't have a great deal to say, make this your resume page -- talk about your qualifications (your skill set and education)

Purpose

To develop the look and feel you want to develop for your personal/business/ministry/educational/portfolio Web site

Tools

  • Word Processor or Layout Application (InDesign)

Requirements

  • A document (done in MS Word or Apple's Pages (.doc, .docx, .pages), a basic text editor (.TXT or .RTF format, or Layout application (InDesign and export as a PDF) with the following information
    • Mission or Artists Statement
    • A basic resume or qualifications page
    • A list of items that will be on your "product"/ "portfolio" page

Submission

Submit all your work in a (compressed) folder titled: f1_1-lastname (ex. f1_1-michaud).
